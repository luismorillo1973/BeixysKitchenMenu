Charred Caprese Salad

Smoky, charred mozzarella with a touch of Sazon seasoning

While Caprese is an Italian classic, the twist of smoky cheese and Latin seasoning pays homage to Venezuela’s love for bold flavors and fusion cooking.

Black Bean & Cheese Empanadas

Golden corn empanadas filled with beans, spices, and cheese

Empanadas are a beloved street food in Venezuela, often enjoyed for breakfast or as a quick snack. In coastal regions, fillings range from fresh fish to sweet plantains.

Patacones con Carne Desmechada

Fried green plantains topped with shredded beef

Patacones (also called tostones) are a staple across Latin America, but in Venezuela, pairing them with juicy, slow‑cooked beef is a weekend tradition for family gatherings.

Hallacas

Traditional Venezuelan tamales filled with beef, pork, olives, raisins, and capers, wrapped in plantain leaves

Hallacas are Venezuela’s most iconic Christmas dish, blending Indigenous, African, and European culinary traditions into a single festive bite.

Churro Cheesecake Bars

Rich cheesecake layered with cinnamon sugar churros

This dessert is a modern fusion—combining Latin America’s favorite fried treat with the creamy decadence of cheesecake. Perfect for sweet endings.

Angel’s Falls: The world’s highest uninterrupted waterfall tumbles more than 3,200 feet in Venezuela’s Canaima National Park. This video evokes its dramatic descent.

Catatumbo Lightning: Captured near Lake Maracaibo, these nocturnal thunderstorms produce near‑continuous lightning, illuminating the sky and inspiring legends.
